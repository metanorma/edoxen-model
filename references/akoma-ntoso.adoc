Akoma Ntoso
===========
Akoma Ntoso (XML "hearts and minds" in Akan) is the OASIS standard
for machine-readable parliamentary, legislative, and judicial
documents. It is the most-cited XML schema for formal proceedings
in the legal/parliamentary world, used by the European Parliament,
Italian Camera dei Deputati, UK Parliament, German Bundestag,
Brazilian Congress, UN System, and dozens of other legislative
bodies.

It is the only major reference that gives us a canonical
*debate record* structure — i.e. "who said what, in what order,
with what outcome" — modeled explicitly. This is the closest
external standard to Edoxen's `Minutes` + `MinutesSection`.

Source: OASIS Standard `legaldocml-akn-core-v1.0` (Akoma Ntoso
Version 1.0, Committee Specification). The official URL is
`https://docs.oasis-open.org/legaldocml/legaldocml-akn-core/v1.0/`
(membership-gated). Wikipedia's article at
`https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akoma_Ntoso` provides a public
summary; the structure below is the well-established canonical
form.


== Definition (per Wikipedia / official abstract)

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akoma_Ntoso

> Akoma Ntoso is a legal document standard designed to serve as a
> basis for modern machine-readable and fully digital legislative
> and judicial processes. This is achieved by providing a coherent
> syntax and well-defined semantics to represent legal documents
> in a digital format. It is designed to be suitable as a common
> exchange format in all parliamentary, legal and judicial systems
> around the world.

The Akoma Ntoso standard defines a set of machine-readable
electronic representations in XML format of the building blocks
of parliamentary, legislative and judiciary documents.

> "(...) defines a set of simple, technology-neutral electronic
> representations of parliamentary, legislative and judiciary
> documents for e-services in a worldwide context and provides
> an enabling framework for the effective exchange of 'machine
> readable' parliamentary, legislative and judiciary documents
> such as legislation, **debate record, minutes**, judgements,
> etc."
> — Akoma Ntoso official abstract

Key adoptions cited:

- European Parliament (and EP's AT4AM amendment editor)
- Italian Camera dei Deputati and Senato
- UK Parliament (post-2017 modernization)
- German Bundestag (LegalDocML.de profile, v1.0 March 2020)
- Brazilian Congress
- African Parliamentary Union (APU) member states
- UN System (AKN4UN profile — Guidelines for UN normative,
  parliamentary and judicial documents)


== Top-level document types (the Akoma Ntoso root elements)

The core Akoma Ntoso XML schema defines several top-level document
types. Each is a specialization of a generic `akomaNtoso` root
element, with its own name and semantic.

|===
| Root element | What it models | Edoxen equivalent

| `<act>`            | A legislative Act (law that has been passed).       | `ResolutionCollection` (close)
| `<bill>`           | A draft law, before passage.                       | (no Edoxen equivalent; pre-adoption)
| `<debateRecord>`   | The verbatim record of a parliamentary debate.     | `Minutes` + `MinutesSection` (close)
| `<debate>`         | A debate (subset of debateRecord; also reused).    | `MinutesSection` (sub-unit)
| `<amendment>`      | A proposed amendment to a bill.                    | (no Edoxen equivalent)
| `<amendmentList>`  | Collection of amendments.                          | (no Edoxen equivalent)
| `<judgment>`       | A court judgment.                                  | (out of scope)
| `<doc>`            | A generic document (catch-all for non-parliamentary).| (no Edoxen equivalent)
| `<statement>`      | A formal statement (e.g., ministerial).            | `MinutesSection` (sub-unit, type=statement)
| `<division>`       | A recorded vote.                                   | `VoteRecord` (the act of voting itself)
|===

Note the central one for Edoxen: `<debateRecord>` is the formal
root element for the proceedings of a parliamentary session. It
IS the document that records "who said what, in what order, with
what outcome, in a sitting that produced these acts/amendments/
divisions". This is the closest external standard to Edoxen's
`Minutes` model.


== DebateRecord structure (the minutes equivalent)

A `<debateRecord>` carries:

- `<preface>` — metadata block (FRBRthis, FRBRuri, FRBRdate,
  references, etc.)
- `<references>` — citations to other Akoma Ntoso documents
- `<debateBody>` — the actual content

`<debateBody>` is organized as:

  <debateBody>
    <debateSection id="intro">
      <speech by="#mp-jones" role="chair">...</speech>
    </debateSection>
    <debateSection id="item-1">
      <speech by="#mp-jones" role="chair">Item 1 — minutes</speech>
      <question by="#mp-smith">Were minutes approved?</question>
      <answer by="#mp-jones">Yes.</answer>
      <motion id="m1">That the minutes be approved.</motion>
      <division id="d1">
        <count>
          <for>10</for>
          <against>0</against>
          <abstentions>0</abstentions>
        </count>
        <outcome>passed</outcome>
      </division>
    </debateSection>
    <debateSection id="item-2">...</debateSection>
    ...
    <summary>
      <p>The session adjourned at 17:00.</p>
    </summary>
  </debateBody>

Key element types and their Edoxen mappings:

|===
| Akoma Ntoso element | Semantics | Edoxen equivalent

| `<debateRecord>`            | The whole meeting record     | `Minutes`
| `<debateBody>`              | Body of the record           | (implicit; `Minutes` body)
| `<debateSection>`           | One section, often keyed by agenda item | `MinutesSection`
| `<speech by="#…" role="…">` | What one person said         | `MinutesSection.speaker` + body
| `<question by="#…">`        | A question posed              | (could become a `MinutesSection` of kind=question)
| `<answer by="#…">`          | An answer given               | (could become a `MinutesSection` of kind=answer)
| `<motion id="m1">`          | A formal motion               | (closest: `Resolution` is the post-vote result; the motion itself is not in Edoxen today)
| `<division id="d1">`        | A formal vote                  | `VoteRecord`
| `<count><for/against/abstentions>` | Tally of the division   | `VoteRecord` aggregate / roll-up
| `<outcome>passed|failed|withdrawn</outcome>` | Result of motion/vote | `VoteRecord.outcome`
| `<summary>`                 | Closing narrative             | (no Edoxen equivalent; could be Minutes.trailer)
| `<references>`              | Cross-references              | `Meeting.resolution_refs[]` + others
|===


== Speech and role

Each `<speech>` carries:

- `by="#person-uri"` — required pointer to a person (TLCPerson in
  the references section)
- `role` — optional role marker (e.g., `chair`, `vicechair`)
- `as="#party-uri"` — optional party affiliation
- `startTime` / `endTime` — timestamps
- body content — can include `<p>`, `<list>`, etc.

This matches what Edoxen Minutes sections need: who, in what
capacity, when, what was said.

Example:

  <speech by="#mp-jones" role="chair" startTime="2026-09-15T14:32:00Z">
    <p>I call this meeting to order.</p>
    <p>The first item on the agenda is approval of the minutes.</p>
  </speech>


== Motion and division

Akoma Ntoso separates *the motion* (what was proposed) from *the
division* (the recorded vote). This is significant for Edoxen
because today Edoxen's `Resolution` conflates "the motion as
adopted" with "the resolution as adopted" — the *proposal* (motion)
isn't separately tracked.

Motion element:

  <motion id="m-2026-001" date="2026-09-15">
    That this Committee approves the proposal as amended.
  </motion>

Division element:

  <division id="d-2026-001" date="2026-09-15">
    <count>
      <for>10</for>
      <against>2</against>
      <abstentions>1</abstentions>
    </count>
    <outcome>passed</outcome>
    <recordedBy>#secretary</recordedBy>
  </division>

Motion-level tracking (whether passed/failed/withdrawn) is exactly
what Edoxen Resolution is missing as a separate concept. Today,
Edoxen conflates the motion's status with the resolution's
adoption.


== Person references and TLCPerson

Akoma Ntoso uses **TLCPerson** (Terminology, Language, and
Citation Person) as the canonical person type. Each person has:

- `id` — referenced via `#…` URI fragment
- `showAs` — display name
- `name` — structured name parts (given, family, prefix, suffix)
- `birth` / `death` — biographical dates
- `contact` — contact info
- `affiliation` — party/organization

A complete parliamentary record then has a top-level `<references>`
section with all people, organizations, and concepts identified
by URI fragments, then `<debateBody>` content references them by
`by="#…"` / `as="#…"` / `to="#…"`.

This is the same glossarist-style pattern Edoxen already uses for
`Localization`. Adopting the TLCPerson structure would unify the
person model across Edoxen Meeting / Resolution / Minutes.


== FRBR naming convention

Akoma Ntoso mandates FRBR-style (Functional Requirements for
Bibliographic Records) naming. Each document carries:

- `<FRBRthis>` — URI of this expression
- `<FRBRuri>` — URI of the work
- `<FRBRdate>` — date of this version
- `<FRBRauthor>` — author of this version
- `<FRBRcountry>` — country code (ISO 3166-1 alpha-2)
- `<FRBRsubtype>` — for the National Council of Provinces etc.

Example:

  <debateRecord>
    <preface>
      <FRBRthis>/akn/za/na/debate/2026-09-15/main</FRBRthis>
      <FRBRuri>/akn/za/na/debate/2026-09-15</FRBRuri>
      <FRBRdate date="2026-09-15" name="sitting"/>
      <FRBRauthor href="#na-secretary"/>
      <FRBRcountry>za</FRBRcountry>
    </preface>
    ...
  </debateRecord>

Maps onto Edoxen's `Meeting.urn` + `Meeting.identifier[]`. The
Akoma Ntoso pattern is more detailed but the structural mapping
is direct.


== What Akoma Ntoso does NOT have (and Edoxen must keep)

1. **Logistics / scheduling.** No concept of when/where/who is
   invited (that's iCalendar's territory). A debateRecord is a
   *retrospective* record, not a *prospective* invitation.

2. **Public-web identity.** No URL/landing-page model, no event
   description for search engines (Schema.org's territory).

3. **Virtual meeting details.** No CONFERENCE / join URL /
   attendee count, because AKN was designed for physical
   parliamentary chambers. Hybrid/hybrid-virtual sessions post-
   2020 have been retrofitted by some AKN implementations but
   the core standard has no schema for them.

4. **Recurrence / series.** AKN documents are individual
   expressions; series relationships are handled at the
   application level (e.g., "all sittings in 2026") by URI
   pattern, not by a native recurrence model.

5. **Capacity / registration / offers.** AKN is post-hoc
   (minutes), not prospective (tickets/registration).

6. **Per-attendee RSVP state.** AKN records who ATTENDED and who
   SPOKE; it does not model "who was invited, accepted, declined,
   delegated" (iCalendar's territory).


== Net contribution to Edoxen

Akoma Ntoso is the *debate-record* layer. It is the only major
reference that gives Edoxen canonical structure for what is
*currently missing* in Edoxen's Minutes model: explicit speech,
motion, division, outcome.

Concrete additions Akoma Ntoso forces onto the Edoxen Minutes model:

- `MinutesSection.kind: Enum {speech, question, answer, motion,
  division, statement, summary}` — replaces the current flat
  list-of-Markdown-blocks model with a typed section model
- `MinutesSection.speaker: Person` (was: implicit in body text)
- `MinutesSection.role: String?` (chair/vicechair/rapporteur;
  from AKN `role` attribute on `<speech>`)
- `MinutesSection.start_time: DateTime`
- `MinutesSection.end_time: DateTime`
- `MinutesSection.outcome: Enum {passed, failed, withdrawn,
  deferred}` for motion/division sections
- `MinutesSection.related_motion: String?` — links division
  sections to their motion sections
- `Minutes.references[]` — TLCPerson + organization registry
  pattern, adopted as a top-level glossary
- `Resolution.motion_separate: Boolean` (or new `Motion` entity)
  — separating "what was proposed" from "what was adopted"

Concrete things Akoma Ntoso does NOT replace in Edoxen:

- iCalendar's scheduling/recurrence/virtual-meeting layer
- Schema.org's public-web identity layer
- Edoxen's existing Agenda/AgendaItem structure (Akoma Ntoso
  doesn't model agenda explicitly — agenda items appear as
  `<debateSection>` keys but without separate agenda semantics)

This gives a complete three-layer coverage:

1. **iCalendar** (RFC 5545 + 7986 + 9253) — logistics, scheduling,
   virtual meeting, recurrence
2. **Schema.org Event** — public-web identity, capacity, status,
   registration
3. **Akoma Ntoso** — parliamentary record structure (speech, motion,
   division, outcome)
