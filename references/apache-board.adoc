Apache Software Foundation Board Minutes
==========================================
The Apache Software Foundation (ASF) Board of Directors meets
monthly via teleconference. Meeting minutes have been published
publicly for every meeting since 1999 — a 25+ year corpus of
formal corporate-board minutes with motions, votes, attendance
rolls, and resolutions.

This is the canonical open-data reference for the *corporate
board meeting* pattern — officers, roll call, officer reports,
committee reports, special orders (formal resolutions), action
items, and adjournment. Edoxen adopts ASF minutes as the
corporate-meeting reference to complement the standards-body
references (which have no "Board Chair / Treasurer / Secretary"
distinction) and the IETF reference (which has no formal
motions/votes).


== Real corpus used

Source: `https://www.apache.org/foundation/board/calendar.html`

Most recent meeting used in this reference:
`board_minutes_2026_05_20.txt` (20 May 2026, 6,380 lines).

The full corpus spans from 1999 to present, all published as
plain `.txt` files under
`https://www.apache.org/foundation/records/minutes/YYYY/board_minutes_YYYY_MM_DD.txt`
(or `.png` for older scanned minutes pre-2005).

A second view, with categorized navigation:
`https://whimsy.apache.org/board/minutes/`


== Verbatim minutes structure (board_minutes_2026_05_20.txt)

  The Apache Software Foundation
  Board of Directors Meeting Minutes
  May 20, 2026

  1. Call to order
  2. Roll Call
  3. Minutes from previous meetings
  4. Executive Officer Reports
  5. Additional Officer Reports
  6. Committee Reports
  7. Special Orders
  8. Discussion Items
  9. Review Outstanding Action Items
  10. Unfinished Business
  11. New Business
  12. Announcements
  13. Adjournment

This 13-section order is the canonical corporate-board agenda,
per Robert's Rules of Order. Edoxen's `Agenda` model can
inherit this directly as a default template.


== Section-by-section mapping to Edoxen

|===
| Apache section | What it contains | Edoxen equivalent

| 1. Call to order | Time, quorum recognition, venue ("via Zoom"), backup channel | `MinutesSection.kind=opening`; `Meeting.virtual[]` populated
| 2. Roll Call | Directors present / absent; Officers present / absent; Guests | `Attendance[]` with `role: director | officer | guest`
| 3. Minutes from previous | Each prior meeting's status (Approved by General Consent / Tabled) | `MinutesSection.kind=approval` referencing prior `Meeting` URN
| 4. Executive Officer Reports | Chair, President, Treasurer, Secretary, EVP, VP, Vice Chair | `MinutesSection.kind=report, role=…`
| 5. Additional Officer Reports | VP W3C, Infra, Travel Assistance, etc. | (same as 4, for non-executive officers)
| 6. Committee Reports | Brand, Legal, Marketing, Fundraising, etc. | `MinutesSection.kind=report, committee=…`
| 7. Special Orders | Formal motions ("Establish the Apache X Project") | `MinutesSection.kind=motion` + `Resolution` with `<Resolution>`
| 8. Discussion Items | Topics for discussion, no vote | `MinutesSection.kind=discussion`
| 9. Review Outstanding Action Items | Past action items + status | `MinutesSection.kind=action_review` + `Deadline`
| 10. Unfinished Business | Tabled items from prior meetings | `MinutesSection.kind=continuation`
| 11. New Business | New items raised this meeting | `MinutesSection.kind=motion` (new) or `discussion`
| 12. Announcements | Non-actionable notifications | `MinutesSection.kind=announcement`
| 13. Adjournment | Time the meeting ended | `MinutesSection.kind=closing`
|===

        Daniel Gruno
        Alin Jerpelea
        Paul King

This is a four-tier attendance structure: Directors (board
members), Executive Officers (paid staff), Additional Officers,
Guests (observers). Edoxen's `Attendance.role` currently has a
single enum (`present` / `absent` / `apologies` / `observer` /
`excused`); ASF data forces a richer structure:

  Attendance {
    person: Person
    role: Enum {
      chair,                  // chair of this meeting
      director,               // voting board member
      executive_officer,      // paid staff (CEO, CFO, ...)
      officer,                // appointed officer (Secretary, Treasurer)
      guest,                  // observer, non-voting
    }
    partstat: Enum { present, absent, apologies, excused }
    affiliation: String?       // PMCs the director represents
  }


== Officer roles — the second tier of corporate governance

The ASF Board has a full officer hierarchy absent from standards-
body references:

  - Board Chair (Sander Striker)              — runs the meeting
  - President (Ruth Suehle)                   — chief executive
  - Treasurer (Craig McClanahan)              — financials
  - Secretary (Matt Sicker)                   — minutes, records
  - Executive Vice President (Daniel Ruggeri)
  - Vice Chair (Justin Mclean)
  - VPs (W3C Relations, Infrastructure, ...)
  - Executive Officers (paid staff)

Edoxen's `Meeting` model has:
  - chair: Person
  - secretary: Person
  - host: String

Missing: president, treasurer, vp, executive_officer, vice_chair.

ASF data forces adding these as optional fields, OR a
generic `Meeting.officers[]: {role, person}` array that
captures all officer roles by name. The array form is
cleaner — it scales to orgs with 3 officers or 30.


== Special Orders (formal motions/resolutions) — verbatim

  7. Special Orders

    A. Establish the Apache Magpie Project

       WHEREAS, the Board of Directors deems it to be in the best
       interests of the Foundation and consistent with the
       Foundation's purpose to establish a Project Management
       Committee charged with the creation and maintenance of
       open-source ...

       Special Order 7A, Establish the Apache Magpie Project, was
       tabled.

    B. Establish the Apache Livy Project

       WHEREAS, the Board of Directors deems it to be in the best
       interests of the Foundation and consistent with the
       Foundation's purpose to establish a Project Management
       Committee ...

       Special Order 7B, Establish the Apache Livy Project, was
       approved by Unanimous Vote of the directors present.

The Special Order pattern is the corporate equivalent of
standards-body resolutions. It carries:

- Identifier (7A, 7B, 7C, …)
- Title ("Establish the Apache Livy Project")
- Preamble (WHEREAS clauses — equivalent to Edoxen `Consideration`)
- Outcome phrase ("approved by Unanimous Vote", "tabled",
  "approved by General Consent")

Outcome-phrase vocabulary (observed across the corpus):

- `approved by Unanimous Vote`
- `approved by General Consent` (no objections)
- `approved by Majority Vote`
- `tabled` (deferred to a later meeting)
- `withdrawn`
- `failed`

Maps onto Edoxen `Resolution.outcome` + new
`VoteRecord.outcome` enum values. Edoxen currently has
`ApprovalType` (`affirmative`, `negative`, etc.) but the
outcome phrasing on a Special Order is a separate concept
(it's about the MOTION's fate, not individual votes).


== WHEREAS clause structure (preamble)

Each Special Order begins with WHEREAS clauses:

  WHEREAS, the Board of Directors deems it to be in the best
  interests of the Foundation and consistent with the Foundation's
  purpose to establish a Project Management Committee charged with
  the creation and maintenance of open-source ...

This is a *finding* or *preamble* — the justification before the
operative clause. Standards-body references use different words
(`considering`, `noting`, `recognizing`) but the structure is
identical.

Direct map onto Edoxen `Resolution.localizations[].considerations`
which today carries "considering / pursuant / noting / taking
into account" sub-types. ASF uses only WHEREAS but the structural
slot is the same.


== Logistics / virtual-meeting details

The opening paragraph of every recent ASF meeting:

  1. Call to order

    The meeting was scheduled for 21:00 UTC and began at 21:02 when
    a sufficient attendance to constitute a quorum was recognized by
    the chair.

            Other Time Zones: https://www.timeanddate.com/worldclock/fixedtime.html?iso=2026-05-20T21:00:00&msg=ASF+Board+Meeting

    The meeting was held via teleconference, hosted by the Secretary
    via Zoom.

    The #asfboard channel on the-asf.slack.com was used for backup.

This is the canonical mixed-mode meeting preamble. Concrete
fields extractable:

- Scheduled start time (UTC)        → `Meeting.date_range.start`
- Actual start time (UTC)           → `Meeting.actual_start` (new)
- Quorum recognition                → (informational; not modeled)
- Venue (teleconference / Zoom)     → `Meeting.virtual_access[]`
- Backup channel (Slack)            → `Meeting.backup_channel` (new)
- World-clock link                  → `Meeting.attached_urls[]`

The "scheduled vs actual" distinction is real and load-bearing
for parliamentary / corporate minutes — standards-body
references don't show it, but every ASF minutes does. New field
proposed: `Meeting.actual_start: DateTime` and
`Meeting.actual_end: DateTime` (already partially in
`ScheduleItem`).


== What ASF data forces onto the Edoxen Meeting model

Concrete additions:

- `Meeting.officers[]: {role, person}` — replaces single
  `chair`/`secretary` with a typed officers array; supports
  chair, president, treasurer, secretary, vp, executive_officer,
  vice_chair, and any org-specific role.
- `Attendance.role: Enum {director, officer, executive_officer,
  guest, observer, attendee}` — richer than current single-axis
  enum.
- `MinutesSection.kind: Enum {opening, roll_call, approval,
  report, motion, discussion, action_review, continuation,
  announcement, closing}` — based on the 13-section ASF order;
  Edoxen today has no `kind` on `MinutesSection`.
- `MinutesSection.committee: String?` — for committee reports
  (Brand, Legal, Marketing, ...)
- `MinutesSection.outcome_phrase: String?` — verbatim outcome
  text ("approved by Unanimous Vote", "tabled", "withdrawn")
- `Meeting.actual_start: DateTime` + `Meeting.actual_end:
  DateTime` (start/end vs scheduled time)
- `Meeting.backup_channel: String?` — secondary comms channel
  ("Slack #asfboard")
- `Meeting.is_quorum: Boolean?` — set at Call to Order
- `Resolution.outcome_phrase: String?` — verbatim outcome text
  from the motion section
- New `Motion` entity (or boolean flag on `Resolution`):
  separates the *proposal* (which may be tabled or withdrawn)
  from the *resolution* (what was adopted)


== What ASF does NOT have (and Edoxen must keep)

1. **Multi-day / multi-track / multi-session.** ASF Board is a
   single 1-hour monthly call, no parallel sessions. The
   multi-day model comes from IETF (see `references/ietf.adoc`).

2. **Detailed agenda with timing.** ASF has a fixed 13-section
   order; agenda items don't carry time budgets or presenters.
   The IETF agenda with durations and presenters is the
   load-bearing reference for that.

3. **Virtual meeting access URIs.** The minutes SAY "via Zoom"
   but the actual join URL is not recorded in the minutes (it's
   in the separate calendar invitation, which is not
   public). iCalendar's CONFERENCE property captures the URIs
   properly.

4. **Conference series / tracks.** The ASF meeting is one of
   ~12 per year, but they're not modeled as a series — each
   meeting has its own identifier. Series modeling (MeetingSeries)
   comes from IETF.

5. **Per-attendee partstat (RSVP).** ASF records attendance
   retroactively (present/absent), not RSVP state (accepted/
   declined/tentative). iCalendar captures the RSVP layer.


== Net contribution to Edoxen

ASF minutes are the *corporate-board formal minutes with motions
and resolutions* reference. Adopting it forces the corporate-
governance layer into Edoxen:

- Officers array (chair/president/treasurer/secretary/vp/...)
- Attendance roles (director/officer/guest/observer)
- 13-section agenda template (Call to Order → Adjournment)
- MinutesSection kind enum
- Outcome phrase as verbatim text on motions
- Actual vs scheduled time tracking
- WHEREAS preamble on formal orders (where Edoxen already
  supports it via `consideration`)

Combined with the other references, Edoxen now has full
coverage of:

1. **iCalendar** — scheduling, recurrence, virtual access
2. **Schema.org Event** — public-web identity, status, capacity
3. **Akoma Ntoso** — parliamentary record structure
4. **IETF** — multi-day, multi-track, multi-session
5. **Apache ASF** — corporate-board formal minutes with motions
6. **ISO/ITU/BIPM/ILO/OIML** — standards-body resolutions (existing)
