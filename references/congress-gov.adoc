US Congressional Meetings and Hearings
======================================

Source: GovInfo.gov (U.S. Government Publishing Office),
the official source for US Congressional publications.

Collection: Congressional Hearings (CHRG)
https://www.govinfo.gov/app/collection/chrg
Metadata schema documentation:
https://www.govinfo.gov/help/chrg

The Edoxen model adopts govinfo CHRG as the canonical
record shape for *government / legislative committee
meetings* — including hearings, markups, and oversight
sessions of House, Senate, and joint committees.


== What a Congressional Hearing is (per GovInfo)

From https://www.govinfo.gov/help/chrg (verbatim):

> A hearing is a meeting or session of a Senate, House,
> joint, or special committee of Congress, usually open
> to the public, to obtain information and opinions on
> proposed legislation, conduct an investigation, or
> evaluate/oversee the activities of a government
> department or the implementation of a Federal law.
> In addition, hearings may also be purely exploratory
> in nature, providing testimony and data about topics
> of current interest.

Coverage:

- GovInfo has select House and Senate hearings for the
  104th Congress (1995–96) forward.
- Most congressional hearings are published two months to
  two years after they are held.
- Not all hearings are released on GovInfo — the publishing
  decision belongs to each committee.

Three properties Edoxen must add (they are structurally
implied but not explicit in govinfo CHRG):

- `witnesses: Person[]` — persons who testified. GovInfo
  records them only inside the PDF; they are not in the
  search-indexed metadata. Edoxen must extract and model.
- `related_legislation: BillRef[]` — bills under
  consideration (joint committee print, related CRPT, or
  bill number mentioned). Not in CHRG schema; common in
  associated CPRT and BILLSTATUS records.
- `transcript_url: String` — separate from the hearing PDF,
  the verbatim transcript (when published).


== Hearing subtype taxonomy (load-bearing for Edoxen `meeting_type`)

The CHRG `hearingsubtype` enumeration is the most
authoritative government-meeting taxonomy we have:

  f   Field         — held outside Washington, at the site
                       of the matter under investigation
  m   Markup        — committee session to mark up
                       (amend) a bill before reporting it out
  o   Oversight     — review of executive-branch activities
  au  Authorization — review of agency authorization bills
  ap  Appropriation — review of agency budget requests
  n   Nomination    — confirmation hearings for executive
                       appointments and judicial nominations
  t   Treaty        — Senate advice and consent on treaties
  g   General       — catch-all for exploratory / informational

This taxonomy is *categorically distinct* from Edoxen's
current `meeting_type.lutaml` enum:

  Edoxen current:                  CHRG-aligned:
  - plenary                        - (no equivalent — chrg
  - working_group                     doesn't have plenary
  - task_group                        meetings; House/Senate
  - ad_hoc                            floor sessions live in
  - joint                             CREC, not CHRG)
  - conference_session

The right move is to extend Edoxen's `MeetingType` with the
CHRG subtypes (and a `floor_session` for CREC). Mapping:

| Edoxen current | CHRG equivalent |
|---|---|
| `plenary`         | (House/Senate floor; lives in CREC) |
| `working_group`   | `g` (General committee hearing) |
| `task_group`      | `o` (Oversight) or `g` |
| `ad_hoc`          | `o` (Oversight, ad-hoc committee) |
| `joint`           | CHRG chamber=`joint` |
| `conference_session` | (no equivalent — this is a misnomer for a plenary-grade constitutional meeting; rename to `general_assembly` per existing Gap 1 from scope discussion) |
| —                 | `m` (Markup)        |
| —                 | `au` (Authorization) |
| —                 | `ap` (Appropriation) |
| —                 | `n` (Nomination)    |
| —                 | `t` (Treaty)        |
| —                 | `f` (Field)         |


== Chamber / Branch / Session (load-bearing for Edoxen)

The Edoxen Meeting model needs to express *legislative
position* explicitly when the meeting is a Congressional
committee session. The CHRG schema provides the canonical
fields:

- `Meeting.legislative.chamber: Enum {house, senate, joint}`
  — directly from `chamber`
- `Meeting.legislative.congress_number: Integer` — from
  `congress` (110th Congress = 2007–2008)
- `Meeting.legislative.session: Integer (1|2)` — from
  `session`
- `Meeting.legislative.branch: Enum {legislative, ...}` —
  from `branch` (almost always `legislative` for CHRG;
  exists for cross-domain future use)
- `Meeting.legislative.committee: String`
- `Meeting.legislative.subcommittee: String`
- `Meeting.legislative.hearing_number: Integer` (from
  `hearingnum`)
- `Meeting.legislative.is_appropriation: Boolean`
- `Meeting.legislative.is_nomination: Boolean`
- `Meeting.legislative.heading: String` ("part 2",
  "volume 1", "errata")

For the broader Edoxen model (post-broadening), this
suggests a new top-level entity:

  Meeting
    legislative: MeetingLegislative[0..1]
      chamber, congress_number, session,
      committee, subcommittee,
      hearing_number, heading,
      is_appropriation, is_nomination,
      related_legislation, witnesses

That keeps the standards-body side (ISO, ITU, OIML, ILO)
unchanged — they have no legislative sub-structure — and
adds the government dimension cleanly. Alternatively the
fields could live directly on `Meeting`, but the `legislative`
sub-object keeps the model clean for non-government meetings.


== What GovInfo CHRG does NOT have

1. **Witness lists as a structured field.** Witnesses are
   only in the published PDF. The `nominee` field exists
   for nomination hearings but is unstructured (just a
   search term). Edoxen must extract witnesses from PDFs
   or accept them as derived/curated input.

2. **Attendee roll-call.** Members of Congress present at
   a hearing appear in `member[]` but only as search terms
   (last name). No structured attendance record, no party,
   no vote record per member.

3. **Vote records.** Committee markups produce votes, but
   the CHRG record carries only the hearing as a whole —
   individual member positions are not exposed.

4. **Agenda items.** Like iCalendar, the CHRG schema
   captures *when* and *where* the hearing happened, plus
   *who* sponsored it, but not the ordered list of bills
   considered or witnesses heard. The agenda lives in the
   committee's own notice, which is often distributed as a
   separate PDF.

5. **Virtual / hybrid attendance.** Congressional hearings
   became hybrid during COVID-19 (2020–2022) and a number
   of committees now routinely allow remote testimony. The
   CHRG schema is silent on this dimension.

6. **Officers beyond chair / ranking member.** CHRG records
   identify the committee (which implies the chair and
   ranking member) but does not surface officers as a
   structured field. Edoxen's `chair` / `secretary`
   abstraction still applies.


== Net contribution to Edoxen

Concrete additions CHRG forces onto the Edoxen Meeting model:

- New enum `LegislativeChamber = {house, senate, joint}`
  (and likely `floor` for CREC)
- New enum `HearingSubtype = {field, markup, oversight,
  authorization, appropriation, nomination, treaty,
  general}` aligned to CHRG codes
- New optional block `Meeting.legislative: MeetingLegislative`
  carrying chamber / congress_number / session / committee
  / subcommittee / hearing_number / heading / boolean flags
- Edoxen's existing `MeetingType` extended (per the mapping
  table above) — `markup`, `appropriation`, `nomination`,
  `treaty`, `field` added as first-class values
- `Meeting.witnesses: Person[]` (new) — for proceedings
  where testimony is given, even outside government
- `Meeting.related_legislation: BillRef[]` (new) — for
  linking to bills, motions, or measures under consideration

The legislative sub-block is the cleanest way to model
"this meeting is part of a recurring committee + Congress"
without polluting the corporate / community / scientific
Meeting shape. Standards bodies and academic conferences
will simply not populate it.


== How Edoxen relates to GovInfo CHRG

- Edoxen `Meeting.legislative.chamber` / `congress_number` /
  `session` / `committee` / `subcommittee` / `hearing_number`
  ↔ CHRG `chamber` / `congress` / `session` / `committee` /
  `subcommittee` / `hearingnum` (1:1)
- Edoxen `Meeting.urn` (existing) ↔ constructed
  `urn:gpo:chrg:{congress}{chamber}{hearingnum}:{heading}`
  (1:1)
- Edoxen `Meeting.source_urls[]` (existing) ↔ CHRG `url`
  (the PDF on GovInfo)
- Edoxen `Meeting.date_range` (existing) ↔ CHRG `dateheld`
  (which is "date or dates on which the hearing occurred")
- Edoxen `Meeting.title` (per-language) ↔ CHRG `title`

This makes Edoxen an interoperable front for CHRG: any
CHRG record can be losslessly mapped into Edoxen, and
Edoxen Meetings can be published back to CHRG-compatible
output (PDF + URN + chamber-coded metadata).
